The Critical Challenge

emergency

Urgency in Cardiology

Heart attacks occur every 10 minutes in Australia. Current standard risk factors—hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes—only partially explain the prevalence of Coronary Artery Disease (CAD).

biotech

Elucidating Residual Risk

We must elucidate mechanisms beyond standard factors. The "residual risk" remains high, requiring a deeper understanding of multi-omic interplay to predict and prevent future cardiac events.

Strategic Significance

ACDC establishes a standardized infrastructure pathway, providing a blueprint for national research programs in collaboration with the Australian BioCommons.

public

International Alignment

Architecture strictly adheres to NIH and GA4GH standards, ensuring Australian research is globally interoperable and competitive.

Scalable Infrastructure

The ACDC framework serves as a reference model for other high-performance clinical data initiatives across the continent.

Project Outcomes & Roadmap

Tracing the trajectory from initial pilot phases to a fully established national data commons.

Phase 1 YEARS 1-2

Improved Researcher Capacity

Initial operations focus on centralizing major Australian cohorts (AusDiab, FIELD, BioHEART) and deploying the discovery portal. This phase drastically reduces the time for researchers to discover and request access to harmonized multi-omic data.

Phase 2 YEAR 4 GOAL

Full Establishment & Linkage

The four-year milestone marks a fully established, sustainable Data Commons. This include seamless international linkage, automated governance workflows, and a mature ecosystem supporting precision medicine clinical trials nationwide.

The Opportunity for Precision

Large, well-phenotyped cohorts provide the power to identify specific markers. By integrating Genomic, Lipidomic, Proteomic, and Metabolomic data, ACDC enables researchers to evaluate risk with unprecedented granularity.

Our Strategic Goals

Building a resilient, sustainable, and interoperable national data commons to transform clinical cardiology.

01

Identify Biomarkers

Systematically identifying diagnostic and prognostic biomarkers for CAD through integrated multi-omic analysis.

02

PRS Performance

Assessing the clinical utility and performance of Polygenic Risk Scores specifically within the Australian population demographic.

03

Global Interoperability

Ensuring ACDC architecture is fully interoperable with international standards including the NIH and Global Alliance for Genomics and Health (GA4GH).
